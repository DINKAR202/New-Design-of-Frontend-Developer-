Overview B.Com LLB

  B.Com LLB is a professional law program which is of five years duration.Aspirants can taught both business and legal topics in the integrated Bachelor of Commerce and Bachelor of Legislative Law (BCom LLB) programme. Business Statistics, Business Communication, Auditing, Economics, etc. are a few of the disciplines that applicants study in the BCom LLB programme. Candidates are also taught legal topics such as contract law, consumer protection law, criminal law, legal terminology, statutory interpretation, and constitutional law in addition to these. After completing BCom LLB, graduates have various career options. They can practice law as advocates, join law firms, work in corporate legal departments, or pursue careers in judiciary, public service, legal academia, or legal research.

Course Duration -

The B.Com LLB is a Five year integrated Course.

Eligibility & Admission

Eligibility Criteria

● Students must have completed their 10+2 / equivalent exam with a minimum aggregate percentage of 45-50% in any stream from a recognized board.

● Qualifying in any one of the Entrance exams is required depending upon the college/university.

Future Scope

Future Scope

Graduates can opt for multiple job roles after completing their BCom LLB. Students can choose to become practicing lawyers and join law firms or establish their own legal practices. They can specialize in specific areas of law, such as criminal law, corporate law, family law, intellectual property law, or environmental law. With experience and expertise, lawyers can handle diverse cases and build a successful legal career.

Employment Opportunities

Graduates with a BCom LLB degree can pursue various career paths in the legal field. Many go on to become practicing lawyers, working in law firms, government agencies, corporate legal departments, or as legal consultants. Others may choose to work in non-legal professions where legal knowledge is valuable, such as business, finance, policy-making, journalism, or advocacy.

Venture into Self-Employment

B.Com LLB graduates also have the option to pursue self-employment and start their own ventures in the legal field. Possibilities of Self-Employment After BA LLB

Higher Studies

BCom LLB graduates can pursue higher studies in law. Many graduates choose to specialize in specific areas of law by pursuing a Master of Law (LLM), or LLD degree. Some BCom LLB graduates may also choose to pursue a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Law for academic or research careers.
